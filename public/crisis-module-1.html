üß† Section 1.1: The Alarm System

"Meet your amygdala ‚Äî your brain's smoke detector. Its job is to keep you alive by detecting threats. The problem? It can't tell the difference between a tiger and a text message."

When it fires, three things happen:

  1. Your thinking brain (prefrontal cortex) goes partially offline
  2. Your body floods with stress chemicals (adrenaline, cortisol)
  3. You get powerful action urges (fight, flee, freeze, or other impulsive behaviors)

This is why you can't 'logic yourself out' of panic. Your logic center is literally impaired.

üåä Section 1.2: The Emotional Wave

"Here's the most important thing to understand: Emotions are temporary. They feel permanent, but they're not. Every emotion follows a predictable pattern - it rises, peaks, and falls. This is called habituation."

The peak usually happens around 10-20 minutes. If you can survive those 20 minutes without acting on destructive urges, the intensity WILL decrease. This isn't positive thinking - it's neuroscience.
